ABOUT

Our project uses the Sloan Digital Sky Survey data to predict galaxy subclasses. Learn about the data, the machine learning model, and how it all works together.


The Sloan Digital Sky Survey provides an extensive catalog of astronomical data.
Our machine learning model processes this data to classify galaxies into 'STARFORMING' or 'STARBURST'
categories based on their photometric characteristics. Explore the data and insights to understand the universe better.

How It Works

Discover how our galaxy classification tool works to provide accurate predictions on galaxy types. Follow these simple steps:

1. Input Data

Enter the photometric data of your galaxy into the form below.
data is essential for ourmodel to make a precise classification.
Ensure you provide all required details for the best results.

2. Submit Your Data

Click the 'Submit' button to send your data to our model.
The submission process transmits your information for analysis.

3. Data Processing

Our advanced machine learning model processes the submitted data.
Trained ontheSloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) dataset, the model
analyzes the characteristics of your galaxy to determine its subclass.

4. Receive Prediction

Once processing is complete, you will receive a prediction indicating
whether thegalaxy isclassified as 'STARFORMING' or 'STARBURST'.
Review the results to gain insights into the galaxy’s type.
